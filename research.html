Research

The questions I care about

My work treats science and innovation as systems we can measure, model, and improve. I combine econometric analysis of large bibliometric and patent datasets with game-theoretic and agent-based modeling to ask how policy choices shape what gets discovered, by whom, and at what cost.

01Science of science

How knowledge is produced

I study the structure of scientific production at scale — collaboration patterns, the diffusion of ideas, the dynamics of novelty and impact. Recent work has appeared in Science, Research Policy, and Science and Public Policy.

02Economics of science

Incentives shape discovery

How do funding structures, career incentives, and institutional design influence research productivity and the direction of inquiry? I look at the mechanisms that turn money and people into knowledge.

03Patent policy

IP systems & innovation

Patent examination, scope, and enforcement decisions ripple through the innovation economy. My research uses large-scale patent data and policy variation to estimate these effects empirically.

04Methods

How I work

i.

Econometric analysis

Causal inference on bibliometric and patent datasets, with attention to identification strategy.

ii.

Game-theoretic modeling

Formal models of strategic behavior among researchers, firms, and policy actors.

iii.

Agent-based simulation

Computational models for systems where heterogeneity and interaction matter more than averages.
